Stretching Movements and Their Importance in Sports

Stretching movements are one of the most fundamental and important parts of sports exercises. Although many people sometimes do not take it seriously, proper muscle stretching can make a big difference in performance, physical health, and injury prevention.

Stretching is actually a movement that helps increase muscle length and improve joint range of motion. When muscles are stretched before and after exercise, blood circulation in those areas increases and oxygen delivery to tissues is done better.

Performing stretching movements before exercise prepares the body for physical activity and reduces the likelihood of injury. At this stage, stretching should be gentle and dynamic; that is, muscles move in a controlled manner, not held static.

After exercise, static or fixed stretching is also very important. In this type of stretching, the muscle is held in a stretched position for a few seconds to reduce tension in it. This helps faster recovery, reduce muscle pain, and calm the nervous system.

Sports psychology research shows that stretching movements not only have physical effects but also calm the mind. During stretching, regular breathing and focus on the body reduce stress and improve mind-body connection. Dr. Rick Hanson, American sports psychologist, says: Stretching is a bridge between a calm mind and a powerful body.

Also, Dr. James Fowler, professional bodybuilding coach, says: An athlete who ignores stretching will sooner or later get involved with their own body. This sentence reminds us that flexibility is one of the most important factors in maintaining muscle and joint health.

In professional training, stretching movements are considered one of the three essential stages of every training session, along with warming up and cooling down. Even in sports such as football, bodybuilding, cycling, and yoga, coaches emphasize stretching exercises a lot.

Physiologically, continuous stretching increases the elasticity of muscles and tendons. This means the muscle can withstand more pressure and the chance of tearing or cramping decreases. On the other hand, these movements improve nerve-muscle coordination by stimulating muscle nerve receptors.

For example, stretching legs before running exercises, or stretching waist and shoulders before bodybuilding exercises, can noticeably improve your performance.

But the important point is that stretching should not be painful. If you feel severe pain during stretching, it means you have gone beyond the safe limit. The goal of stretching is liberation and preparation, not pressure.

Finally, remember that stretching is necessary not only for the body but also for the mind. Just as the body needs to be released from pressure, the mind can reach peace with a few minutes of calm stretching and deep breathing.
