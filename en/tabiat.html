Scientific research has also confirmed this phenomenon. Studies show that being in green spaces reduces the level of cortisol (stress hormone), regulates heart rate, and improves the activity of the prefrontal cortex of the brain, which is associated with thinking and decision-making. Even short-term presence in nature is enough. For example, before class, one can sit for a few minutes in a quiet place with a few trees, engage in deep breathing, and let the mind calm down. Between classes, a few minutes of walking in green space helps restore focus.

If we go to nature with friends, it is better not to let our minds get involved in scattered conversations alongside companionship. The time is useful when everyone has a common goal: experiencing peace and silence. Even studying simpler parts of the lesson in nature can increase learning efficiency; because a calm mind becomes more receptive and creative.

Ultimately, nature does for the human mind what sleep does for the body: reconstruction, repair, and balance.
